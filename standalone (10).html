Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our AMC Part One preparation program

The AMC Part One Examination is a multiple-choice question (MCQ) examination conducted by the Australian Medical Council for international medical graduates seeking registration to practice medicine in Australia. It assesses medical knowledge and clinical decision-making across different medical disciplines.

Our program includes weekly live classes, recorded sessions, extensive practice questions, mock exams, and personalized feedback. We cover all major topics in the AMC exam blueprint with a focus on high-yield content and test-taking strategies.

Most students prepare for 3-6 months, depending on their background and time commitment. Our structured curriculum is designed to be completed in 16 weeks, with additional time for revision and mock exams.
